## 4. THE FOUR REAL REASONS STARTUPS DON'T RAISE FUNDS

*(Rajat Sir's diagnosis from 600+ founders — this is the hook)*

*(Use on website as: "Why Most Startups Never Raise Funds — And It Has Nothing To Do With Your Idea")*

**For the public website — use these four:**

1. **Wrong Investors** — Running behind everyone for investment. Not able to identify the right investor for their specific business. Not every investor is your investor.

2. **Wrong Approach** — Approaching with a 3-year plan and lots of passion. Telling stories but missing the critical point — why does THIS specific investor need to work with THEM?

3. **No Godfather Offer** — Most founders don't know what to offer or how to close. They have no closing strategy. They panic when interest is shown.

4. **Weak Documentation — but not what you think** — Founders waste time on pitch decks and financials. The real weakness is legal and deal documentation — what to sign with investors or customers. Term sheets, shareholder agreements, deal terms. This is the blind spot that kills deals AFTER the investor says yes.

**Additional truths — use for content, social media, speaking. NOT for website:**

- Bad ownership design — cap table structured emotionally, scares investors away

- Cannot negotiate — accept bad terms or lose the deal

- Building castles in the air

- Thinking investment is a shortcut to making money

- All gas, no substance — collapse under hard questions

- Running behind every website and every person

## 12. THE FILTER SYSTEM

### Three Types of Time-Wasters

1. The Curious — want free knowledge, disappear after 2 hours

2. The Conditional Buyer — "Guarantee results, then I'll pay"

3. The Unserious — pay, don't implement, blame Rajat Sir

### Three-Layer Filter

Layer 1 — Website language pushes away the unserious

Layer 2 — Stage 0 itself (can't complete 7 questions = won't approach 50 investors)

Layer 3 — Active booking step after email (passive interest drops off here)

### The Positioning Shift

- NEVER say "Book a Session"

- ALWAYS say "Apply to Work with Rajat Sir"
